Explore Pembrokeshire

Discover the enchanting beauty of Pembrokeshire, a coastal gem nestled in southwest Wales. With its stunning landscapes, rugged cliffs, pristine beaches, and charming seaside villages. The area is a paradise for outdoor activities such as hiking, kayaking, and wildlife watching.

Pembrokeshire also boasts a rich history, with historic castles and landmarks to explore. Immerse yourself in the welcoming local culture, enjoy fresh seafood, and experience the tranquility of this picturesque destination. Pembrokeshire offers a diverse range of experiences for an unforgettable travel adventure.

The Swan Inn

The 200-year-old Swan Inn is one of the most spectacularly located pub/restaurants in Pembrokeshire boasting a water's edge location, a rustic but elegant interior and a modern British menu of exceptional quality. The commitment to seasonal and local produce is strong with the emphasis on the freshest fish and seafood caught.

Point Road, Little Haven, Haverfordwest, SA62 3UL

The Shed Bistro

A relaxed & informal fish & chip bistro in the renowned village of Porthgain, situated on the Pembrokeshire coast path, serving caught and local fish & shellfish; landed daily & served within hours, where possible. It is a firm favourite among locals and visitors alike. Inside, local artwork adorns the walls and coastal decor adds to the cosy atmosphere. Also serve fresh coffee, teas and homemade cakes

56 Llanrhian Rd, Porthgain, Haverfordwest SA62 5BN

The Farmer's Arms

Traditional Public House In The Centre Of The UK’s Smallest City, St. Davids. A cosy comfortable setting in the shadow of the cathedral. Provides a fantastic menu with many seasonal Welsh dishes and a large variety of specials. Has an open fire which makes it actually rather warm on winters’ nights and a room where you can play pool and darts.

14-16 Goat St, St Davids, Haverfordwest SA62 6RF
